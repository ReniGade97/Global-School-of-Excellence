About Us

The Global School of Excellence for Gifted Students will address the needs of gifted and talented students from the age of three years to eighteen years of age. The educational institution will be a co-education governed by a cadre of trained professionals. The educational institution will be registered by the Ministry of Education, Youth and Information and the Jamaica Independent Schools Association.

The aim of this institution is to establish a school for gifted and talented students, using Science, Technology, Engineering, Mathematics, and Virtual Education as the educational foundation for identifying, nurturing and developing the innate abilities of its students and assisting them to ultimately achieve their highest potential. What is Giftedness?

It is very important to know that: intellectual giftedness can be described as the intelligent ability that is significantly higher than the average intelligence. It is also classified as that special characteristic that children possess, requiring them to be very motivated highlighting major differences in school programme. In addition, intellectual giftedness can also include high ability, or specific abilities. Furthermore, IQ tests can be used to determine the ability of children. Depending on the type of test that is used: mildly gifted children score from 115 to 129, moderately individuals achieve a score of 130 to 144, highly gifted persons score range from 145 to 159, while, exceptional gifted individuals score from 160 to 179, and profoundly gifted children score somewhere within the range of 180. Also, gifted children are those individuals who show high performance capability in areas such as: intellectual, creativity, artistic, leadership capacity, or specific academic fields, and who require services or activities not usually provided in the regular schools in order to fully develop their capabilities. While it is the belief of some persons that giftedness can be achieved through the process of nurturing, however, the overwhelming consensus states that it is present at birth, an inherited trait. In addition, the chances are very high, that if one, or both parents are gifted, there is the strong likelihood that a child, or siblings will also be gifted.


Listed are some of the common characteristics of a gifted child:
  • Unusual alertness, even in infancy.
  • Rapid learner; puts thoughts together quickly
  • Excellent memory
  • Unusually large vocabulary and complex sentence structure for age
  • Advanced comprehension of word nuances, metaphors and abstract ideas
  • Enjoys solving problems, especially with numbers and puzzles
